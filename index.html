Student Visa in Japan

The Student Visa allows international students to study in Japan at various educational institutions, such as language schools. This visa requires students to be enrolled in a recognized program and meet specific financial and academic requirements.

SSW Visa

The SSW Visa is a program introduced by the Japanese government to address labor shortages in various industries. It allows foreign workers with specific skills and expertise to work in Japan for an extended period. This visa offers a pathway for skilled workers to gain employment in sectors such as agriculture, construction, etc.

Training Visa in Japan

The Training Visa allows foreign nationals to undergo training in Japan to acquire skills and techniques that can be difficult to obtain in their home country. This visa is aimed at individuals who will participate in training programs provided by Japanese companies, organizations, or other entities.

The Jobs

The Specified Skilled Worker visa was implemented in April 2019 in a bid to address Japan's serious labor shortages by accepting experienced foreign workers with specific expertise and skills in 14 designated industries:

Agriculture Industry

The Japanese SSW Visa (Specified Skilled Worker) for the Agriculture Industry is designed to attract skilled foreign workers to meet the growing demand in Japan's agricultural sector. Workers under this visa are employed in various roles, such as crop production, livestock farming, and agricultural machinery operation. The visa allows individuals to work in Japan for up to five years.

Construction Industry

The Japanese SSW Visa (Specified Skilled Worker) for the Construction Industry is aimed at addressing labor shortages by attracting skilled foreign workers to Japan's construction sector. Workers under this visa are engaged in roles such as building construction, civil engineering, and construction machinery operation. The visa allows individuals to work in Japan for up to five years, helping to support the nation's infrastructure projects and urban development initiatives.

Care Giver Industry

The Japanese SSW Visa (Specified Skilled Worker) for the Caregiver Industry is designed to bring in skilled foreign workers to support Japan’s growing need for elderly and disability care. Workers under this visa assist with daily living activities, personal care, and health management in various care facilities. The visa allows individuals to work in Japan for up to five years, helping to address the shortage of caregivers as the country's aging population increases.

Food Industry

The Japanese SSW Visa (Specified Skilled Worker) for the Food Industry aims to attract skilled foreign workers to meet the labor demands in Japan’s food production and processing sector. Workers under this visa are employed in roles such as food manufacturing, processing, quality control, and packaging. The visa allows individuals to work in Japan for up to five years, supporting the country's food supply chain.

Machine Parts & Tooling Industry

The Japanese SSW Visa (Specified Skilled Worker) for the Machine Parts and Tooling Industry is designed to attract skilled foreign workers to fill labor shortages in Japan's manufacturing sector. Workers under this visa engage in tasks such as machine operation, part assembly, and maintenance of industrial tools and equipment. The visa allows individuals to work in Japan for up to five years, supporting the production of high-precision machinery and contributing to the nation's industrial growth.
